About Organ Donation

Understanding the life-saving impact of organ donation and transplantation

What is Organ Donation?

Organ donation is the process of giving an organ or tissue to help someone who needs a transplant. Organs that can be donated include heart, kidneys, liver, lungs, pancreas, and intestines. Tissues that can be donated include corneas, skin, bone, heart valves, and blood vessels.

Types of Donation

Living Donation

When a living person donates an organ (like a kidney or part of liver) to someone in need.

Deceased Donation

When organs are donated from someone who has died, as indicated by their decision or family consent.

Did You Know?

One organ donor can save up to 8 lives and enhance the lives of many more through tissue donation.

Time Critical

Every 10 minutes, someone is added to the national transplant waiting list.

Donor vs Recipient

Donor

  • Must be 18+ years old
  • Good physical and mental health
  • Free from chronic diseases
  • Willing to save lives
  • Medical fitness certificate required

Recipient

  • Organ failure diagnosis
  • Medical evaluation required
  • Blood type compatibility
  • Tissue matching needed
  • Regular follow-up care

Myths vs Facts

Myth

Doctors won't try to save my life if they know I'm an organ donor.

Fact

Medical professionals are dedicated to saving every patient's life, regardless of donor status.

Myth

I'm too old to be an organ donor.

Fact

People of all ages can be organ donors. Medical evaluation determines eligibility.

Myth

My religion doesn't allow organ donation.

Fact

Most major religions support organ donation as an act of charity and saving life.

Eligibility Criteria

Basic Requirements

  • Age 18 years or older
  • Good general health
  • No active cancer or HIV
  • No chronic diseases affecting organs
  • Sound mental health
  • Medical fitness certificate

Medical Evaluation

All potential donors undergo thorough medical evaluation to ensure safety for both donor and recipient.

Confidential Process

All medical information is kept confidential and used only for donation purposes.

Legal & Safety Information

Legal Framework

Organ donation is regulated by national and international laws to ensure ethical practices and protect both donors and recipients.

Safety Measures

Strict medical protocols ensure donor safety throughout the donation process with minimal risks.

Informed Consent

Donors must provide informed consent, understanding all aspects of the donation process.

Privacy Protection

All donor information is protected by privacy laws and shared only with authorized medical personnel.
